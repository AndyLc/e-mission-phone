AccessMap Research App (Beta)

Powered by E-Mission: Data driven carbon emission reduction

Introduction and Purpose

This app aims to research travel behavior to improve experience of other OpenToAll related services. It is powered by E-Mission, which is part of a travel behavior research platform developed at the University of California, Berkeley. The platform development is being led by K. Shankari under the supervision of Prof. David Culler.

What we collect

We automatically track your location, accelerometer, device-generated activity recognition, and battery usage. We also allow you to optionally enter your experiences and feedback during travel through the app.

How we associate it with you

You log in to the app using a valid OpenToAll account. We map the account to a unique UUID and associate all collected data with the UUID. In general, the mapping between the account and the UUID will only be made available to the lead researchers for the platform. The reason we use an account is so that we can ensure that your data is only available to somebody logged in as you, and remains available even if you switch phones.

Who gets to see it

Individually identified, real-time data is only available to researchers for the study. It will not be used for marketing or advertising purposes.
